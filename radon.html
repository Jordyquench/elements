─── RADON ───

The last element we're going to talk about is Radon, Being one of the heaviest gases, radon tends to accumulate in low lying areas such as house basements, but is a gas that is very hard to detect.

Similar to hydrogen (H), helium is usually found as a gas and has no color or smell. Helium is found everywhere in the Universe ands outermost electron orbital is full with two electrons. You can also find helium in compressed air tanks, lasers, and as coolants in nuclear reactors.

USES

Though it is considered a heath hazard in the United States, radon gas is frequently used as a therapeutic treatment for arthritis in many European countries.

DISCOVERY

In 1900, German physicist Friedrich Ernst Dorn discovered radon. In 1908, William Ramsay and Robert Whytlaw Gray isolated Radon.

COOL FACT

More of a dangerous fact for the last element, Radon gas is the 2nd leading cause of lung cancer in North America
